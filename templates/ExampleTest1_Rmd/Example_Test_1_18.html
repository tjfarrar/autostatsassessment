  4. Question

    Imagine a lottery game in which 53 sequentially numbered balls are in a machine, and 7 main balls are drawn without replacement. A eighth ball is then also drawn, which is called the bonus ball. A player plays the game by selecting seven numbers between 1 and 53 on a card. The player can win a prize based on how many numbers on her/his card match those that came out of the machine (whether as main balls and/or the bonus ball). The table below describes the top seven prize categories.

    1 seven main balls
    2 six main balls plus bonus ball
    3 six main balls without bonus ball
    4 five main balls plus bonus ball
    5 five main balls without bonus ball
    6 four main balls plus bonus ball
    7 four main balls without bonus ball

    Calculate the probability of winning a Level 5 prize. Express your answer as \(I\) where the probability is ‘1 in \(I\)’, \(I\) being rounded to the nearest integer.


    Solution

    Let \(M=m\) be the event of having \(m\) of one’s selected numbers come out on the main balls \((m=0,1,2,\ldots,7)\). Let \(B=b\) be the event of having \(b\) of one’s selected numbers come out on the bonus ball \((b=0,1)\). Then, the probability of winning a Level 5 prize can be expressed as,

    \[\begin{eqnarray*} \Pr\left(M=m\cap B=b\right) &=& \Pr\left(M=m\right)\Pr\left(B=b|M=m\right)\\ &=& \frac{{n \choose m}{N - n \choose n - m}}{{N \choose n}}\left[\left(\frac{n-m}{N-n}\right)^{b}\left(\frac{N-2n+m}{N-n}\right)^{1-b}\right] \\ \Pr\left(M=5\cap B=0\right)\\ &=& \frac{{7 \choose 5}{46 \choose 2}}{{53 \choose 7}} \left(\frac{7 - 5}{46}\right)^{0}\left(\frac{53 - 2(7) + 5}{46}\right)^{1-0} \\ &=& 1.3487\times 10^{-4} \\ &\approx& \text{1 in 7414} \end{eqnarray*}\]

    Thus the required answer is 7414.

  6. Question

    The discrete random variable \(X\) has the following probability mass function:

    \[\begin{eqnarray*} f_X(x)&=\begin{cases} \frac{3}{22} + \frac{1}{22} x \text{ for } x = 1, 2, 3, 4 \\ 0 \text{ otherwise} \end{cases}\end{eqnarray*}\]

    Find the expectation of \(X\). Do the question by hand, then scan and upload the question as an attachment here. Your name and student number should be visible on the page. Numerical answers should be correct to four significant digits or given as fractions.


    Solution

    Marking Scheme: \(\rule{1.5ex}{1.5ex}\) indicates awarding of one mark.

    \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathrm{E}(X)&= \displaystyle\sum_{x=1}^{4} x f_X(x) \rule{1.5ex}{1.5ex} \\ &= 1 f_X(1) + 2 f_X(2) + 3 f_X(3) + 4 f_X(4) \rule{1.5ex}{1.5ex} \\ &= \frac{30}{11} = 2.727 \rule{1.5ex}{1.5ex} \end{eqnarray*}\]
